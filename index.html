Big Buddha + Muay Thai Fight Night

🥊

🥊 Muay Thai 8:00pm
8am

🍳 Breakfast

Hotel Breakfast

Good start — Big Buddha is best in the morning before the midday heat. Dress modestly for the temple visit (shoulders and knees covered — sarongs available to borrow on-site).

9:30am
🚕
🚕 Transfer

Hotel → Big Buddha, Nakkerd Hills

~30–40 min drive south from Patong up into the hills. Scenic road winds up through rubber plantations. Taxis/tuk-tuks approx 400–500 THB each way.

10:15am
🙏
🙏 Sightseeing

The Big Buddha — Phuket's Most Iconic Landmark

The magnificent 45-metre tall white marble Mingmongkol Buddha sits atop Nakkerd Hill and can be seen from across Phuket. Entry is free — donations welcome.

Walk up to the base for close-up views. See the smaller surrounding statues, prayer halls and meditation spaces. The 360° panoramic view from the hilltop is absolutely breathtaking — Patong, Kata and Karon beaches all visible on a clear day.

👗 Temple dress code: cover shoulders and knees. Free sarong loans available at the bottom of the steps.
11:30am
🏯
🏯 Temple

Wat Chalong — Phuket's Most Important Temple

Just 10 min drive from Big Buddha. Phuket's largest and most revered Buddhist temple. Ornate spires, detailed murals, monks in residence. Deeply peaceful and culturally fascinating. Free entry.

🕯️ You can make an offering of incense and flowers at the main altar — a beautiful experience for the whole family.
1:00pm
🍛
🍛 Lunch

Local Thai Lunch Near Chalong

Chalong area has some fantastic local restaurants away from the tourist strip. Try Khao Tom Boran or any street stall — authentic and cheap. Amazing Thai green curry, massaman, and pad kra pao.

2:00pm
🏘️
🏘️ Old Town

Stop: Phuket Old Town en Route Back

On the way back from Chalong, ask your driver to drop you in Phuket Old Town (~10 min from Wat Chalong). Spend 1–1.5 hours exploring before heading back to the hotel — it's perfectly timed before Fantasea prep.

Phuket Old Town is a UNESCO-recognised Sino-Portuguese neighbourhood full of beautifully preserved colourful shophouses, vibrant street art, hipster cafes, night market stalls and quirky boutiques. Thalang Road and Soi Romanee are the most photogenic streets. Great for a coffee and a wander.

📸 Soi Romanee is the most Instagrammable street in Phuket — pastel shophouses, bougainvillea, street art. A 30-minute wander covers the highlights.
3–5pm
🏊
🏊 Relax

Afternoon — Pool & Rest

Hotel pool, quick nap, get the kids sorted before heading out. Big Friday night energy incoming.

7:00pm
🍜
🍜 Dinner

Quick Dinner Near the Stadium

Grab a meal near Bangla Boxing Stadium before the fights. Street food, Thai BBQ, or a nearby restaurant — keep it casual and don't eat too much. You'll be on your feet cheering!

8:00pm
🥊
🥊 Fight Night

Muay Thai Boxing — Bangla Boxing Stadium

Friday night is fight night. Bangla Boxing Stadium on Soi Bangla hosts professional Muay Thai bouts with a typical card of 8–10 fights over 2.5–3 hours.

Ringside seats put you right in the action — Thai trainers and gamblers cheering, traditional Sarama music setting the pace, fighters throwing elbows and knees. Kids absolutely love it and it's totally family-safe.

💰 Ringside 1,500–2,000 THB · Standard 1,000 THB. Book at the gate or online. Cold beers available throughout.
10:30pm
🍹
🍹 Friday Night

Post-Fight Friday — Bangla Road

It's right there. Friday night on Bangla Road is electric — the biggest night of the week. Celebrate the fights with cocktail buckets, live bands and the full Patong experience at its peak.
